PCA concept
Concept Focus
  • WHAT: Dimensionality reduction technique that finds new axes (principal components) that capture maximum variance.
  • WHAT (continued): Projects high-dimensional data to lower dimensions while preserving as much information (variance) as possible.
  • WHY: Reduce features from 1000 to 50 while keeping 95% of information.
  • WHY (continued): Enables visualization of high-dim data.
  • WHERE: Feature reduction, visualization, noise removal, preprocessing
  • KEY: "Find fewer dimensions that capture the most information"
  • HOW TO APPLY: Prepare small dataset -> apply method -> evaluate -> compare with simple baseline.
  • USE IN PRACTICE: You use it when building a baseline model and improving results.
  • COMMON MISTAKE: Always validate with metrics. A single prediction example is not enough.
  • EXAMPLE: Simple example: train on tiny data and print one prediction plus one metric.
