Dropout
Separate Subtopic Learning Modules
Subtopic 1
Dropout
Concept Focus
  • WHAT: Regularization technique that randomly "drops" (sets to zero) a fraction of neurons during each training step.
  • WHAT (continued): Each neuron is dropped with probability p (typically 0.2-0.5) During inference: all neurons active, weights scaled
  • WHY: Prevents co-adaptation — neurons can't rely on specific other neurons.
  • WHY (continued): Forces redundant representations.
  • WHERE: Between dense layers in neural networks
  • KEY: "Randomly turn off neurons during training to prevent overfitting"
  • HOW TO APPLY: Define network -> pass input -> compute loss -> update weights.
  • USE IN PRACTICE: You use it when training ANN/CNN/RNN models for complex patterns.
  • COMMON MISTAKE: Print tensor shapes often. Most beginner bugs come from shape mismatch.
  • EXAMPLE: Simple example: one forward pass and print output shape.
  • REMEMBER THIS: Randomly turn off neurons during training to prevent overfitting

Batch normalization basics
