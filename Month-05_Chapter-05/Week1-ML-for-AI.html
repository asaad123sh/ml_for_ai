Subtopic 1
Naive Bayes theory
Concept Focus
  • WHAT: Probabilistic classifier based on Bayes' theorem with "naive" assumption that all features are independent of each other given the class.
  • WHAT (continued): P(class|features) ∝ P(class) × P(f1|class) × P(f2|class)...
  • WHY: Very fast, works great with high-dimensional data (text).
  • WHY (continued): Requires very little training data.
  • WHERE: Text classification, spam detection, document categorization
  • KEY: "Uses Bayes theorem + independence assumption for fast classification"
  • HOW TO APPLY: Start with tiny numbers -> compute step-by-step -> verify with code.
  • USE IN PRACTICE: You use it to understand training logic, metrics, and optimization.
