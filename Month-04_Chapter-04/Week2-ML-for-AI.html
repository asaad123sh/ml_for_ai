Gradient descent
Separate Subtopic Learning Modules
Subtopic 1
Gradient descent
Concept Focus
  • WHAT: Optimization algorithm that iteratively moves parameters in the direction OPPOSITE to gradient to minimize loss.
  • WHAT (continued): w = w - learning_rate × gradient Like a ball rolling downhill to the lowest point
  • WHY: THE fundamental optimization algorithm for training ALL neural networks and many ML models.
  • WHY (continued): Without it, models cannot learn from data
  • WHERE: Training every neural network, logistic regression, linear regression with large data
  • KEY: "Move downhill on the loss surface to find the minimum"
  • HOW TO APPLY: Start with tiny numbers -> compute step-by-step -> verify with code.
  • USE IN PRACTICE: You use it to understand training logic, metrics, and optimization.
  • COMMON MISTAKE: Understand meaning first, formula second. Do not memorize without intuition.
  • EXAMPLE: Simple example: use small integers so every step can be checked manually.
  • REMEMBER THIS: Move downhill on the loss surface to find the minimum
Example 1: Core Example (Python)
# Gradient descent intuition on f(w) = w^2
w = 5.0
learning_rate = 0.1

for step in range(5):
    grad = 2 * w               # Derivative of w^2
    w = w - learning_rate * grad
    print(f'Step {step + 1}, w = {w:.4f}')
Expected Output
Expected output:
Step 1, w = 4.0000
Step 2, w = 3.2000
Step 3, w = 2.5600
Step 4, w = 2.0480
Step 5, w = 1.6384
Example 2: one manual gradient update (Python)
w = 2.0
lr = 0.05
grad = 2 * w
w_new = w - lr * grad
print('Updated weight:', w_new)
Expected Output
Expected output:
Updated weight: 1.8
